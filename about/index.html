About

SHLabs builds instruments for the modular world.

SHLabs is a small independent studio making music software of all kinds — modules for VCV Rack today, with standalone apps and VST3 / AU plugins (compatible with Ableton Live and other DAWs) on the way. Whatever the format, we design instruments that put real math on the panel — sequencers, generative processes, gate logic and stochastic sources — drawn from the older tradition of analog computing, where a calculation was something physical and visible rather than hidden behind function calls.

The catalog is organized into module families, each with its own character and visual language: Mashina, Lucida, Atmos, Rikoshet and Stochast. Some modules are free; others are paid releases that fund continued development. Every one of them is built to the same standard — real processes under the hood, seedable so you can recall a patch you love, and panels designed to be patched and played, not just admired.

If a module earns a place in your rack or makes an idea click into place, the most useful thing you can do is patch with it and tell someone about it.

How it started

A founding story.

SHLabs began as evenings spent patching, asking a simple question: what if the maths that usually disappears inside code were laid out as something you could turn, patch, and hear? The first modules were built to scratch that itch — a sequencer here, a generative source there — and they kept growing into families.

Today SHLabs is a one-person studio that ships polished, dependable instruments to the VCV Rack community — and increasingly beyond it, as standalone apps and DAW-ready VST3 / AU plugins join the catalog. New families and updates are released as they're ready.
