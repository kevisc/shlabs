Rhythm · gates · multitap

Rikoshet

A growing series of multitap gate and rhythm modules. The idea is simple: take one input clock, send it through a configurable array of taps and gate-logic combinators, and get back a rich rhythmic polyphony that would otherwise take a rack-row of cabling to assemble by hand. Patch one in for instant counterpoint without losing the pulse of the original clock.

The idea

A multitap delay takes a single input and returns several time-offset copies of it. Rikoshet modules apply that principle to gates: one trigger in, an array of derived triggers out — each at a configurable phase, each routable through a logic combinator (AND, OR, XOR, modulo, swing, polyrhythm).

The result: drum-machine grooves from a single clock; ratcheted envelopes from a single trigger; counterpoint from a single melody line. The Rikoshet philosophy is "one signal in, many deliberately-related signals out", which is a different design stance than the usual "ten LFOs in, one mix out" of modular workflows.

DESIGN INVARIANTS
  • · One input clock per module
  • · N configurable taps (typically 8)
  • · Per-tap phase, probability, gate-length
  • · Polyphonic output cable preserving tap order
  • · Optional gate-logic recombiners
  • · Magenta family-stripe under header

The four modules

GATE

Rhythmic gate

Tempo-synced amplitude gate — 14 musical subdivisions, morphable shape, pulse width, and a stereo offset that slips the right channel for width.

PINGPONG

Stereo multitap

Ping-pong taps bouncing across the stereo field, with per-tap timing and feedback for cascading echoes and rhythmic bounces.

MULTITAP

The core engine

One trigger in, an array of derived gates out — each at a configurable phase, probability, and gate-length, on a polyphonic output cable.

BLEND

Recombiner

Mixes and gate-logics taps (AND / OR / XOR / modulo) into new composite rhythms — the counterpoint stage of the series.
